\chapter*{Preface}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Preface}

These notes were written for a reading seminar on K.~Kodaira,
\emph{Complex Manifolds and Deformation of Complex Structures, I} (1968). Their
main aim is to fill in, by hand, the derivations and details that the book
omits.

\section*{Principles for these seminar notes}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Prove what is written in the text; reconstruct each claim in one's own
        words.
  \item Abbreviate notation and anything else clear from context as convenient.
  \item Prioritize understanding: explain every point that is omitted or glossed
        over.
  \item Reconstruct proofs oneself, or revise the flow of the argument as
        needed.
  \item Write down as many questions and supplementary remarks as possible
        (kept separately from the preparation notes).
  \item Prepare presentation notes (ideally, so that one can present even
        without any notes at hand).
\end{enumerate}
